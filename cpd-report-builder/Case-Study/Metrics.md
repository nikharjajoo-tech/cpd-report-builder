# Metrics and business impact

## Headline results

| Metric | Before | After | Change |
|---|---|---|---|
| First-draft report time | 2 to 4 months | 3 to 5 days | ~90 to 95% faster |
| Document ingestion limit | ~100MB | ~300MB | 3x scalability increase |
| Documents processed per engagement | Manual, fragmented | 500 to 600 | Unified retrieval |
| Team delivering | — | 15 cross-functional members | Full delivery ownership |
| Client stakeholders aligned | — | 6 | Continuous alignment |

## Why these numbers are defensible
The turnaround figure came from comparing the firm's historical manual timeline against measured platform delivery times once ingestion and generation were live. The ingestion scaling figure came directly from structured threshold testing that identified the exact failure point before and after the engineering fix.

## Secondary indicators
- **200+ Jira stories delivered across 11 sprints**, evidence of predictable Agile execution at scale rather than ad-hoc delivery.
- **Validation agent flag rate**, tracked as an ongoing quality signal rather than a one-time launch metric.

## What I'd track differently next time
Reviewer edit rate per AI-generated chapter as a standing dashboard metric, to make draft quality trends visible over time rather than anecdotal. See [Lessons Learned](./Lessons-Learned.md).
